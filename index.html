About

Background

I am now an Assistant Professor at CUNY City Tech. From 2021-2025, I was a Ritt Assistant Professor at Columbia University. Before that, I completed my PhD in Mathematics at Northwestern University in Spring 2021 under the guidance of Ben Weinkove. My research interests are in complex geometry and partial differential equations, specifically, on complex geometric flows and constant scalar curvature problems.

My research is partly supported by an NSF standard grant DMS-2404845.

Research Focus

My research is centered around studying canonical metrics in complex geometry. In particular, I am interested in the existence problem for constant scalar curvature Hermitian metrics using the tools of elliptic PDEs as well as geometric flows.
